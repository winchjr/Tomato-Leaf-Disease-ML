Background

  • Tomato Leaf Image data set
  • From Kaggle: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ashishmotwani/tomato
  • Over 32k images, with 11 categories (1 healthy, rest diseases)
  • Images already split ~80/20 for train test
  • Classification is our goal

Some Examples of the Data

Our Goal

  • Correctly classify images with 95% accuracy in one of 11 categories
  • Using Neural Net or K-means cluster algorithm
  • Optimize the algorithms as needed to get to 95%

Technical Details/Shape of Data

  • 255 * 255 pixel images
  • RGB
  • Images randomly rotated
  • Some in-the-wild, some in-the-lab
  • Wide variety of quality, contrast, brightness, etc
  • No declared single source

More Examples of the Data

Neural Net

  • Trying all 8 default tensor flow optimizers
  • Tested with 128 neurons for all layers, except for the final test
  • Tested 1, 2, 3 hidden layers, and a fourth test with only 32 neurons and 1 hidden layer

Unexpected K-means results

  • 121 clusters is better than 11 clusters?
  • Visualization is difficult, and hard to interpret its meaning
  • Accuracy is 50% better when there are 11^2 clusters
  • Again, computational challenges are preventing me from going further

Discussion

  • Computational limits were preventing further exploration
  • Did not reach our goal of 95%- nowhere close
  • Our elbow graph with k-means clustering seemed to suggest its accuracy had peaked
  • Neural nets, more neurons and more layers, seem to be the best avenue for further improvement in accuracy, and did not seem to be done improving
  • Really great dataset- more optimization needed

Further Neural Net improvement

  • Nadam, Adam, and Adamax performed the best out of all 24 neural net tests (all above 50%)
  • Nadam and Adamax are variants of the Adam optimizer
  • Try denser neurons first, then add more layers
